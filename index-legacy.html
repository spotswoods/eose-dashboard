Frontier Power USA — newly formed Cerberus joint venture (May 13, 2026)

What it is. Stand-alone development & investment company to build, own, and operate a portfolio of long-duration battery storage projects deploying Eos' Z3 zinc bromide systems, targeting Independent Power Producer (IPP) status.

Why it matters. Solves the chicken-and-egg of LDES financing by combining (i) Eos' tech, (ii) Cerberus capital + operating muscle, and (iii) a Technology Performance Insurance (TPI) wrap from Ariel Green that lets project lenders treat Z3 output as financeable.

Cerberus equity:$100M committed Eos target contribution:~$150M* Anchor reservation:2 GWh firm capacity Pipeline target:Multi-GWh across DC, utility, industrial TPI underwriter:Ariel Green (specialty) Cerberus lock-up:Extended through YE 2026

*Subject to Eos' ability to raise funding and third-party approvals.

Risk factors — bear thesis cheat sheet
RiskWhat to watch
Cash burn vs. shelf timing$152M cash decline Q4'25 → Q1'26. At current burn, the S-3ASR shelf is a when, not if. Watch for follow-on offering size and pricing relative to last trade.
Cerberus structural dominanceCerberus holds Series B preferred ($583M), warrants ($203M related-party), and convertible notes ($113M). Series B remeasurement was +$779M in Q1'26 alone. Conversion math drives common dilution.
Customer concentrationIn Q1'26, 93.3% of revenue came from customers each >10% of total. Loss/delay from any one of them moves guidance materially.
Frontier funding executionFrontier Power USA needs Eos to fund ~$150M. Release says "subject to ability to raise funding" — explicit dependency on capital markets access.
Margin trajectoryGM still −78%. Path to break-even requires both Line 2 ramp and Z3 unit economics holding up. Watch fixed-cost absorption per Cube shipped.
RTE vs. Li-ion economics~75–80% RTE vs. ~90% Li-ion. Eos wins only where duration >6h or where safety/lifetime offset the round-trip gap. Watch project mix.
IRA / 45X policy risk$21.4M grant receivable; 45X PTC accrued as COGS offset. Policy reversal would compress GM by several hundred bps.
